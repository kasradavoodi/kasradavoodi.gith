Stress Detection and Quality Assessment

Objective: creating a stress detection method for pulse oximeter of Zistel company. Firstly, we are working on a binary classification. After that, we aimed for multi class classification (stress, non-stress, rest, happy, etc.)
Method: We are assessing multiple machine learning approaches (SVM, XGBoost, KNN, DT), MLP and LSTM. We implemented a two-step preprocessing approach consisted of peak/valley detection and a XGBoost model for quality assessment in series. Firstly, input signal goes through a denoising procedure. After that, peaks and valleys are detected and given to the XGBoost model. The model assesses the quality of segments based on desired time period and a desired minimum threshold for high quality segments. Finally, feature extraction is implemented on the remaining segments and then given to the classifier model (stress /non stress).
Software Tools: Matlab, Python, scikit learn, Tensorflow, Zistel Pulse oximeter, nRF, STM32
